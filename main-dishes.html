Main Dish

Ema Datshi

Bhutan's iconic national dish combining chilies and cheese.

30 mins Spicy

Ingredients:

  • 6-8 green chilies (sliced)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 1 cup Bhutanese cheese
  • 2 tbsp butter
Instructions:
  1. Slice chilies lengthwise and remove seeds if desired.
  2. In a pot, combine chilies, sliced onions, and water.
  3. Bring to a boil and simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Add cheese and butter, stirring until melted.
  5. Season with salt to taste.
  6. Simmer until the sauce thickens.
Tips:
  • Use fresh green chilies for best results
  • Traditional Bhutanese cheese can be substituted with feta

Kewa Datshi

A comforting potato and cheese dish beloved throughout Bhutan.

25 mins Mild

Ingredients:

  • 2 potatoes (thinly sliced)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 1 cup Bhutanese cheese (or feta)
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt to taste
Instructions:
  1. Peel and slice potatoes into thin rounds.
  2. In a pot, combine potatoes, sliced onions, and water.
  3. Bring to a boil and cook until potatoes are tender.
  4. Add cheese and butter, stirring until melted.
  5. Season with salt to taste.
  6. Simmer until sauce thickens.
Tips:
  • Use waxy potatoes for best texture
  • Add chilies for extra heat if desired

Shakam Datshi

A hearty dried beef and cheese stew that's perfect for cold mountain nights.

40 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 200g dried beef (shakam), cut into small pieces
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 2 green chilies (sliced)
  • 1 cup Bhutanese cheese
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt to taste
Instructions:
  1. Soak dried beef in warm water for 30 minutes.
  2. Cut beef into small pieces and boil until tender.
  3. Add sliced onions and chilies, cook for 5 minutes.
  4. Add cheese and simmer until melted.
  5. Season with salt to taste.
  6. Cook until the sauce reaches desired consistency.
Tips:
  • Pre-soaking the beef reduces cooking time
  • Add more water if sauce becomes too thick

Phaksha Paa

A delicious pork and radish dish that's a staple in Bhutanese households.

45 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 300g pork (sliced)
  • 1 cup radish (sliced)
  • 2 dried red chilies
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp ginger (grated)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • Salt to taste
Instructions:
  1. Heat oil in a pan and add ginger and garlic.
  2. Add pork and cook until browned.
  3. Add onions and dried chilies, cook until onions soften.
  4. Add radish and cook until tender.
  5. Season with chili powder and salt.
  6. Simmer until pork is fully cooked and tender.
Tips:
  • Use fatty pork for better flavor
  • Adjust chili quantity to taste

Jasha Maru

A flavorful spicy chicken curry that's popular across Bhutan.

30 mins Spicy

Ingredients:

  • 300g chicken (cubed)
  • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp ginger (grated)
  • 2 green chilies (sliced)
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Salt to taste
Instructions:
  1. Heat oil in a pan and sauté ginger and garlic.
  2. Add chicken and cook until browned.
  3. Add onions and cook until translucent.
  4. Add tomatoes, green chilies, and chili powder.
  5. Season with salt and cook until tomatoes break down.
  6. Simmer until chicken is fully cooked.
Tips:
  • Use boneless chicken for faster cooking
  • Garnish with fresh cilantro

Sikam Paa

Traditional dried pork dish often reserved for special occasions.

40 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 200g dried pork (sikam)
  • 3-4 dry red chilies
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 1 tbsp ginger (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tsp Sichuan pepper (optional)
  • Salt to taste
Instructions:
  1. Soak dried pork in hot water for 15 minutes.
  2. Cut into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Heat oil and add ginger, garlic, and Sichuan pepper.
  4. Add pork and cook until heated through.
  5. Add onions and dried chilies.
  6. Season with salt and cook until onions are soft.
Tips:
  • Sichuan pepper adds authentic flavor
  • Can be made spicier by adding more chilies
