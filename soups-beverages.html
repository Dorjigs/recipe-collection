Bathup (Bhutanese Noodle Soup)

A hearty noodle soup perfect for cold Bhutanese evenings.

45 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 200g wheat flour
  • 100g minced meat (beef or pork)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 1 tbsp ginger (grated)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (sliced)
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 4 cups meat stock or water
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander for garnish
Instructions:
  1. Make dough with wheat flour and water. Let rest for 15 minutes.
  2. Roll dough thin and cut into small square pieces.
  3. In a pot, saut√© onions, garlic, and ginger until fragrant.
  4. Add minced meat and cook until browned.
  5. Add tomatoes, chilies, and stock; bring to a boil.
  6. Add the dough pieces and simmer until they float to the top.
  7. Add butter and salt; simmer for 5 more minutes.
  8. Garnish with coriander and serve hot.
Tips:
  • Make the dough pieces uniform in size for even cooking
  • Use beef or pork stock for richer flavor
Beverage

Suja (Butter Tea)

Traditional Bhutanese butter tea served throughout the day.

15 mins Mild

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons black tea leaves
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/4 cup milk
Instructions:
  1. Boil water in a pot and add tea leaves. Simmer for 3-4 minutes.
  2. Strain the tea into a wooden container or blender.
  3. Add salt, butter, and milk.
  4. Blend or churn vigorously until frothy.
  5. Pour into cups and serve hot.
Tips:
  • Use high-quality butter for best results
  • Traditional churning gives the best texture
Beverage

Ara (Traditional Rice Wine)

Traditional homemade rice wine often served during ceremonies and festivals.

7 days Mild

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice
  • 1 tbsp yeast starter (marchang)
  • Water
Instructions:
  1. Wash rice thoroughly and soak for 6 hours.
  2. Cook rice until soft but not mushy.
  3. Cool rice to room temperature.
  4. Mix in yeast starter thoroughly.
  5. Transfer to a clean airtight container.
  6. Cover and let ferment for 5-7 days in a warm place.
  7. Once fermented, strain or distill as per preference.
  8. Serve at room temperature or warm.
Tips:
  • Use glutinous rice for best results
  • Maintain consistent temperature during fermentation
