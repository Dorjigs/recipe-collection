Side Dishes

Traditional Bhutanese accompaniments and pickles

Side Dish

Goen Hogay

A refreshing cucumber salad that pairs perfectly with Bhutanese curries.

15 mins Medium

Ingredients:

  • 1 cucumber (sliced)
  • 1 tsp chili flakes
  • 1 tbsp spring onions (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp coriander (chopped)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp oil
Instructions:
  1. Slice cucumber into thin rounds
  2. Mix with chili flakes and salt
  3. Add chopped spring onions and coriander
  4. Drizzle with oil and toss gently
  5. Let sit for 5 minutes before serving
Tips:
  • Use fresh, crisp cucumbers
  • Adjust chili flakes to taste
Condiment

Ezay

A fiery chili condiment that's served with almost every Bhutanese meal.

20 mins Very Spicy

Ingredients:

  • 5-6 dried red chilies
  • 2 fresh red chilies
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (crushed)
  • 1 small piece ginger (grated)
  • 1 tbsp Bhutanese cheese (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp vegetable oil
Instructions:
  1. Soak dried chilies in hot water for 15 minutes
  2. Grind dried and fresh chilies together
  3. Mix in chopped onion, garlic, and ginger
  4. Add crumbled cheese if using
  5. Season with salt and oil
  6. Mix well and let rest for 30 minutes
Tips:
  • Store in an airtight container
  • Will keep for up to a week in the refrigerator
Side Dish

Lomba

A traditional fermented turnip pickle that adds tang to Bhutanese meals.

4 days Medium

Ingredients:

  • 500g turnips (peeled and sliced)
  • 2 tbsp salt
  • 1 tbsp turmeric powder
  • 2-3 dry red chilies (crushed)
  • 1 tbsp mustard seeds
  • Water (as needed)
Instructions:
  1. Place sliced turnips in a bowl and add salt
  2. Mix well and let sit for 2 hours
  3. Drain excess water and squeeze turnips
  4. In a clean jar, add turnips, turmeric, chilies, and mustard seeds
  5. Mix well and cover with lid
  6. Ferment at room temperature for 3-4 days
  7. Stir daily for even fermentation
Tips:
  • Use clean, sterilized jars
  • Store in refrigerator after fermentation
